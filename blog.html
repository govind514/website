5 Common Physiotherapy Myths Debunked

Published on 10 August 2025

Many people have misconceptions about physiotherapy, which can prevent them from seeking the care they need. At Mehar Physiotherapy Clinic, we believe in empowering our patients with knowledge. Here are five common myths about physiotherapy, debunked:

Myth 1: Physiotherapy is only for injuries and accidents.
Fact: While physiotherapy is crucial for recovering from injuries, it's also a proactive way to improve your overall health. It can help with posture correction, managing chronic conditions like arthritis, and improving athletic performance. Think of it as a way to both recover and prevent future problems.
Myth 2: I need a doctor's referral to see a physiotherapist.
Fact: In India, you do not need a doctor's referral to see a qualified physiotherapist. You can directly book an appointment with a physiotherapist for an assessment and treatment plan. This allows you to get the help you need sooner.
Myth 3: Physiotherapy is painful.
Fact: The goal of physiotherapy is to relieve pain, not cause it. While some exercises and movements might cause mild discomfort as you work to restore function, your physiotherapist will work with you to manage any pain and ensure you're comfortable with your treatment plan.
Myth 4: Physiotherapy is just a series of exercises.
Fact: Exercise is a key component of physiotherapy, but it's much more than that. A physiotherapist's treatment plan may also include manual therapy, education, and advice on lifestyle changes. It's a holistic approach to your health.
Myth 5: I can just find exercises online and do them myself.
Fact: While there are many resources available online, a "one-size-fits-all" approach to physiotherapy is not effective and can even be dangerous. A qualified physiotherapist will assess your specific needs and create a personalized treatment plan.

The Importance of Ergonomics in Preventing Workplace Injuries

Published on 10 August 2025

In today's world, many of us spend long hours at a desk, which can lead to a variety of musculoskeletal issues. Poor posture and a non-ergonomic workspace can cause back pain, neck stiffness, and repetitive strain injuries. This is where ergonomics comes in.

What is Ergonomics?

Ergonomics is the science of designing and arranging things so that people can use them easily and safely. In the workplace, this means creating a workspace that is tailored to your body and the tasks you perform.

Why is Ergonomics Important?

  • Reduces Pain and Discomfort: A well-designed workspace can significantly reduce the strain on your body.
  • Prevents Injuries: By promoting good posture and reducing repetitive strain, ergonomics helps prevent injuries.
  • Improves Productivity: Comfort and reduced pain help you focus better and work more efficiently.

Simple Ergonomic Tips for Your Workspace:

  • Adjust your chair: Feet flat on the floor, knees at 90°, back supported.
  • Position your monitor: Top of the screen at or slightly below eye level.
  • Keep keyboard & mouse close: Elbows at 90°, wrists neutral.
  • Take regular breaks: Stretch or walk every 30–60 minutes.

At Mehar Physiotherapy Clinic, we can provide a detailed ergonomic assessment and personalized recommendations to help you create a safer and more comfortable workspace.

Recovering from a Sports Injury: A Physiotherapist's Guide

Published on 10 August 2025

Whether you're a professional athlete or a weekend warrior, a sports injury can be a frustrating setback. The right approach to recovery is crucial for getting back to your sport safely and preventing re-injury. Here's a guide from a physiotherapist's perspective:

1. The RICE Principle (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation):
  • Rest: Avoid activities that cause pain.
  • Ice: Apply for 15–20 minutes several times a day.
  • Compression: Use a bandage to help reduce swelling.
  • Elevation: Keep the injured limb elevated above heart level.
2. Seek a Professional Assessment: Get a proper diagnosis from a physiotherapist to assess the extent of your injury and plan your recovery.
3. Gradual Rehabilitation: Includes pain management, range of motion exercises, strengthening, proprioception, and sport-specific training.
4. Listen to Your Body: Avoid rushing recovery — progress safely under guidance.
5. Focus on Prevention: Address underlying factors with warm-ups, cool-downs, strengthening, and proper technique.

If you've suffered a sports injury, the team at Mehar Physiotherapy Clinic is here to help you get back in the game safely and confidently.
